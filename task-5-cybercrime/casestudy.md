## Cyberbullying Case Study

Cyberbullying refers to the use of digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, and online forums to harass, threaten, or humiliate an individual. It commonly affects students and young adults who are active online.

A typical cyberbullying incident often begins with a disagreement or misunderstanding. The offender may start by posting negative comments, spreading rumors, or sharing embarrassing images or messages. Gradually, the behavior escalates as more individuals may join in, either by sharing the content or adding further abusive comments. Victims are often targeted repeatedly, making it difficult for them to escape the situation.

For example, a college student might become the subject of online ridicule after a personal photo is shared without consent. The post gains attention, leading to trolling, abusive messages, and public humiliation. This continuous harassment can severely impact the victim’s mental health.

The consequences of cyberbullying can be serious, including anxiety, loss of confidence, academic decline, and social withdrawal. In extreme cases, it may lead to long-term psychological distress.

This case highlights the importance of responsible online behavior and the need to create a safe and respectful digital environment for all users.
